RESPONSE TO REVIEWERS (INGLÊS):
================================================================================

We thank the reviewer for this constructive observation. We acknowledge that 
the original Introduction, while contextualizing the relevance of EEG/fNIRS 
technologies, did not explicitly articulate the research question guiding 
this review.

To address this concern, we have revised the final paragraphs of the 
Introduction to include:

1. **Explicit research question:** We now state the central question driving 
   this scoping review: "How can commercially available portable EEG and fNIRS 
   devices be systematically characterized and compared to support informed 
   selection for neuroscience and neurorehabilitation research?"

2. **Problem statement:** We have added a sentence emphasizing that device 
   selection represents a genuine methodological challenge. The rapid expansion 
   of the commercial neurotechnology market, combined with heterogeneous 
   technical specifications and the absence of standardized reporting, creates 
   a "paradox of choice" that complicates decision-making for researchers and 
   clinicians.

3. **Clarification of study design:** We explicitly note that this scoping 
   review does not test a hypothesis but instead addresses a mapping and 
   comparative question. The goal is to provide an evidence-based framework 
   for device selection rather than to evaluate the superiority of specific 
   devices or technologies.

These revisions ensure that the research problem is tightly framed and that 
readers understand the scope and purpose of the review from the outset.

[OPÇÃO 1 - Parágrafo completo para inserir antes do último parágrafo atual]

"Despite their growing potential, selecting the most appropriate brain 
monitoring device remains a major challenge for researchers and clinicians. 
The commercial landscape has expanded rapidly, encompassing numerous 
manufacturers and models with heterogeneous technical specifications, 
including differences in signal quality, channel count, electrode or optode 
design, wireless architecture, data accessibility, and integration 
capabilities. This diversity, coupled with the absence of standardized 
technical reporting, hinders direct comparison between systems and complicates 
the alignment of device performance with specific experimental or therapeutic 
goals. As a result, decision-making often becomes complex and time-consuming, 
resembling the paradox of choice, where an excess of alternatives may obscure 
optimal selection.

This scoping review addresses the following research question: How can 
commercially available portable EEG and fNIRS devices be systematically 
characterized and compared to support informed selection for neuroscience 
and neurorehabilitation research? Rather than testing a hypothesis, this 
study provides a structured mapping and technical comparison of the current 
neurotechnology market, offering an evidence-based framework to guide 
device selection based on methodological and operational requirements."


[OPÇÃO 2 - Versão mais curta para inserir]

"Given the proliferation of commercial brain monitoring devices with 
heterogeneous specifications and the lack of standardized comparison 
frameworks, device selection has become a methodological challenge in 
itself. This scoping review addresses the question: How can commercially 
available portable EEG and fNIRS devices be systematically characterized 
to support informed selection for neuroscience and neurorehabilitation 
research? This work does not test a hypothesis but provides a comprehensive 
mapping and technical comparison to guide evidence-based device selection."
